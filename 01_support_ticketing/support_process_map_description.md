## Process overview

The workflow contains the following stages:

1. **Customer email**  
   The customer reports a problem, question or request by email or another support channel.

2. **Ticket created**  
   The request is logged in a helpdesk system as a ticket with basic information such as customer, summary, category, priority and status.

3. **Triage**  
   L1 Support classifies the ticket, checks impact, assigns priority and decides what should happen next.

4. **Troubleshooting**  
   L1 Support asks clarifying questions, checks basic settings, tries to reproduce the issue and collects evidence such as screenshots, browser details or steps to reproduce.

5. **Escalation**  
   If the issue cannot be solved by L1 Support, the ticket is escalated to L2 Technical Support with clear reproduction steps, impact and environment details.

6. **Engineering review**  
   If the problem looks like a product bug, incident or technical limitation, Product or Engineering reviews the issue.

7. **Resolution**  
   The customer receives a fix, workaround, explanation or confirmation that the issue has been solved.

8. **Follow-up**  
   The support or customer success team confirms whether the customer is satisfied and whether the issue can be closed.

9. **Knowledge base update**  
   If the issue is likely to happen again, the team updates internal or customer-facing documentation.

## Roles

### L1 Support

L1 Support owns the first response and basic troubleshooting.

Typical responsibilities:

- create or update the ticket,
- classify category and priority,
- check customer impact,
- ask for missing information,
- reproduce simple issues,
- provide known fixes or workarounds,
- escalate clearly when needed.

### L2 Technical Support

L2 Technical Support handles deeper technical investigation.

Typical responsibilities:

- check logs,
- review integrations,
- validate technical errors,
- confirm whether the issue is a bug,
- prepare clear escalation notes for engineering.

### Product / Engineering

Product and Engineering handle product-level or code-level issues.

Typical responsibilities:

- investigate confirmed bugs,
- review incidents,
- assess product limitations,
- deploy fixes,
- confirm technical root cause.

### Customer Success

Customer Success focuses on the relationship and long-term customer experience.

Typical responsibilities:

- follow up with important customers,
- identify adoption risk,
- explain next steps in business-friendly language,
- support renewals and account health.

## Why this process matters

A structured support process helps the team avoid chaos and repeated questions.  
A good ticket should contain enough information for another person to continue the work without starting from zero.

This is especially important in SaaS support, where tickets often move between Customer Support, Technical Support, Product and Engineering.

---

## Good escalation checklist

Before escalating a ticket, L1 Support should include:

- clear problem summary,
- customer impact,
- priority,
- steps to reproduce,
- expected result,
- actual result,
- environment details,
- screenshots or logs if available,
- what has already been tested,
- recommended next action.
